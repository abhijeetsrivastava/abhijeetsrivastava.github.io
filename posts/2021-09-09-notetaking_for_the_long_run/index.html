Notetaking for the long run

September 9, 2021 3-minute read

In tech world, one would want a second brain. Yes right! if you haven’t heard of second brain it is just a personal data storage where one could query for data. This second brain helps you focus on things that are important to you and to your work.

There is the Zettelkasten method of notetaking which I find very helpful. I am not trying to reinvent the wheel, but just laying out the ideas which I think are most helpful during and after notetaking.

Types of Notes

One would want to take notes during standups, meetings, tech notes, or even notes from the latest book one is reading. All the types of notes would categorize into two types

  1. Temporary Notes
  2. Permanent Notes

With these types of notes, I would like to have a couple of functionalities

  1. Quick search: A notetaking system is no good if one cannot search for previous notes
  2. Easy notes: Taking notes should be easy, and a no thought process.
  3. Note linking: Similar notes should be linked for review later.
  4. Tagging: Categorizing and tagging one’s notes are a great way to keep your notes organized.

Steps for note taking

The prerequisite for any note is that your first understand the thing you are going to take a note of. In order for this the first step that I go through is to take notes in either Ipad or paper with some mind maps and key concepts to be able to visualize the thing I am trying to understand.

The next step is, to convert the notes to a digital format which I can of course query when the time comes. I have certain category that I am interested in and keep different projects for each. I convert my notes in mark-up format (org mode) and then use org roam for giving me quick search and note linking capability.

The notes that I do not want to keep for a long time, I mark them with a certain tag and whenever I feel like I can retire the tag I search my notes and just delete them.

Apps that I use

No matter the apps you use it should provide the features that you think would help you the most. I use the following apps

  1. I use Emacs and orgroam to take my digital notes
  2. I use One note/Apple notes in Ipad for handwritten notes
  3. Earlier I used to write my notes in Markdown using Vim or Obsidian. But now due to personal preference I have moved to Emacs.

Outro

Notetaking is actually great in any field of work. It acts like a second brain that helps you make sense of most things. Sometimes my notes are just runbooks which I personally create to follow for my daily work. It also helps me capture ideas from books which I have read.

No matter the method, I would encourage everyone to take notes and try to learn as much!

Cheers to great learning!
