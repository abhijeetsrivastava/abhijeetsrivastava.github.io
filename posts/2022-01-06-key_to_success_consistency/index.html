Key to success: Consistency

January 6, 2022 2-minute read
success

Intro

I am going to write a few blog posts on things I consider are most important for success. This is part 1 of this series.

Everyone needs to define meaning of success, what it means. A poor guy might want money, ill person would want health, a student might want good marks and job. Everyone has their own definition. So it is important to define one’s own definition of success.

Premise

Key to success: Consistency

There are many key components of success. We are going to explore one of it and what it means.

There are two ways how people approach any project/goal.

People who want something might just start working sporadically and hope to get it. That sporadic motivation in them leads to spend time to figure out what they need to do in order for them to get what they want. If they have the answer already they spend time on working towards it. A student might one day want to master calculus and start solving questions from the material they already have.

Then there are people who need something. They work towards their goal consistently until they see a glimpse of how it is taking them close to their goal or fail in their attempt and get ready to try again with a new plan.

Try and fail until you succeed.

One cannot be the person in the first scenario and working only casually towards a goal. One needs to be consistent. A spark lasts split second unless it gets stuck to a fuel(plan) and then needs consistent effort to keep the fire going.

This is the first step.

Planning phase

Let’s say your long term goal is to clear an exam. You need to have a plan and be consistent with it.

The plan should be easy and achievable with other things going in your life. Studying 4-5 hours a day will give you success, albeit you are consistently putting the effort.

This is the only solution to achieve success. There are others that piggyback on this idea. We can achieve success, if we are consistent.

Conclusion

This is simply one the most important factor in life. I use to think that planning is the most important part for success, while true there is no ultimate plan. One needs to experiment with plans and resources but be consistent in your efforts for success.
